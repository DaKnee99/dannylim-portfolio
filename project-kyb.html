REDUCING THE PAINS OF KYB ONBOARDING

Designed a streamlined digital KYB/KYC onboarding experience for Philippine market expansion, transforming manual email-based processes into an intuitive e-form.

Scope

  • Replaced manual document collection with digital experience
  • Designed for Philippine KYB/KYC compliance requirements
  • Supported multi-session completion for up to 12 document uploads
  • Project paused before launch due to market expansion halt

UI/UX Design  |  6 weeks  |  Product, Engineering, Compliance

The Problem

Spenmo's Philippine expansion required compliant KYB/KYC onboarding, but the existing process was entirely manual—sales collected documents via email, operations verified compliance, endless back-and-forth. Traditional compliance forms overwhelm users with all requirements upfront, provide no progress visibility, and don't account for users lacking documents on hand.

The Solution

Progressive disclosure design with sticky navigation, dynamic document requirements, and auto-fill functionality. The form supports flexible completion since users rarely have all documents immediately available.

Key Design Features

1. Dynamic Document Requirements

Form shows only relevant fields based on business type—sole proprietorships see different requirements than corporations. Reduces cognitive load by hiding irrelevant sections.

2. Sticky Side Navigation

Users can submit up to 12 documents. Returning users immediately see:

  • Which documents are complete (✓)
  • Which are incomplete (○)
  • Which need attention (⚠)

Direct navigation—click to jump to specific upload field. No scrolling to find their place.

Sticky Navigation — Status Indicators

3. Auto-Fill Functionality

Pre-populates applicant details and company information already collected during sales outreach. Reduces form fields and signals we respect their time.

The Document Upload Scenario

The Challenge

Without clear status visibility, returning customers lack immediate clarity on what's submitted. They'd scroll through 12 upload fields trying to remember what they already completed.

The Solution

Sticky navigation stays anchored to top and side. Users visualize all submission statuses at a glance and jump directly to incomplete uploads.

Impact

  • Reduces cognitive effort (no mental tracking)
  • Reduces memory load (visual status replaces remembering)
  • Reduces interaction friction (direct navigation vs. scrolling)
  • Increases feeling of support (guided vs. lost)

Cross-Functional Collaboration

Worked with compliance team to integrate mandatory legal language and requirements without destroying UX—used clear hierarchy, contextual placement, and progressive disclosure to make dense compliance language digestible.

What I Learned

The biggest challenge wasn't regulatory complexity—it was making mandatory compliance feel helpful rather than burdensome. Reframing compliance as user support ("we're helping you understand what you need") led to contextual help text explaining WHY documents were needed.

Regulatory constraints can inspire creative solutions. The constraint of "must collect 12 documents" pushed me to ask: How do we make this manageable? The answer was structural—progressive disclosure, persistent navigation, status tracking—not prettier buttons.
