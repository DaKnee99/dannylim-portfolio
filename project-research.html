FINDING OUT HOW ACCOUNTANTS WORK

Conducted user research with 10 SME accounting heads to uncover bill payment workflows, shaping Spenmo's product roadmap and pricing strategy.

Impact

  • Interviewed 10 companies (50–200 FTE) through in-person sessions
  • Uncovered that SMEs use 3–5 tools for bill payments (not 1–2 as expected)
  • Identified payment scheduler, banking integrations, and accounting software integrations as top priorities
  • Revealed gap between valued features and willingness to pay, informing pricing strategy

User Research  |  4 weeks  |  Product team, C-suite

The Problem

Spenmo's leadership needed detailed understanding of how 50–200 FTE companies handle bill payments to build competitive features and identify monetizable pain points. Current assumptions about workflows were insufficient for strategic product decisions.

The Solution

90-minute research sessions combining semi-structured interviews (35 min) with feature prioritization activities (20 min). This captured both qualitative insights (the "why") and quantitative data (the "how much they'd pay").

Data Synthesis

Research Approach

Participant Recruitment

  • Cold outreach to SME businesses (50–200 FTE)
  • Screening: Accounting heads or senior finance decision-makers
  • 10 in-person interviews, 60 minutes each

Interview Structure

Part 1 — Semi-structured interview

  • Day-to-day workflows
  • Pain points and bottlenecks
  • Tools currently used
  • Busiest periods and challenges

Part 2 — Feature prioritization activity

  • Participants ranked spend management features
  • Allocated hypothetical budget (100%) across features
  • Revealed relative value of features
Feature Prioritization — Axes: Usage Likelihood vs. Willingness to Pay

Key Findings

1. Fragmented Workflows

Companies use 3–5 tools (Excel, accounting software, banking platforms, email, some hardcopy), not 1–2 as expected.

2. Bill List Creation = Biggest Pain

Finance teams identified this as the most tedious task and strongly desired automation.

3. Card Payments: It's About Control

Teams don't prefer cards—not due to fees, but lack of control over transaction timing and amounts.

4. The Spreadsheet Paradox

Despite wanting workflow software, teams resist retiring spreadsheets due to control, customizability, and familiarity.

Product Impact

Research directly influenced roadmap priorities:

  • Payment scheduler (automates tedious list creation)
  • Banking integrations (provides needed control)
  • Accounting software integration (bridges gap without forcing spreadsheet retirement)

Strategic positioning: Frame as workflow enhancement, not spreadsheet replacement, to reduce adoption resistance.

What I Learned

The compliance vs. convenience gap: What accountants valued (convenience features) wasn't always what they'd pay for (compliance and control). Stated preferences and revealed preferences (budget allocation) can diverge significantly.

Spreadsheet attachment is emotional. Years of customization create institutional knowledge that feels irreplaceable. Solutions must acknowledge this attachment rather than dismiss it as inefficiency.
